How to Find Your Real Values

Life purpose isn't handed to you from outside — it's built from your values. And your real values aren't what you say matters. They're what you actually do.

By Karan Jugran, Living Foundations · Part of the Clarity series

Ask most people what they value and they'll give you a polished answer: family, health, growth. But watch how they spend their time and money and a different picture often emerges. Values live in behaviour, not in words. If you say you value health but never move your body, health isn't high on your value scale yet — no matter what you tell yourself.

What a value really is

A value is something you do automatically — no motivation required. That's the test. You don't have to talk yourself into it; you just do it. So to find your values, you don't introspect about ideals. You look at the evidence of your own life.

Watch your actual behaviour, not your stated beliefs. Where your time, money, and energy go without anyone pushing you — that's your true value system revealing itself.

The 5 clarity questions

These five questions take about 25 minutes total. Do them every day for at least two weeks, and don't reread yesterday's answers — start fresh each time. After two weeks, the answers that keep repeating are your true values.

  1. The money question. If you had a large sum to spend in one year (savings set aside), how would you divide it? Keep allocating until it's all spent.
  2. Unlimited wishes. If a genie granted unlimited wishes, what would you wish for? No filter, no judgment.
  3. Risky rewards. What do you deeply want but haven't done because fear is in the way? Name both the reward and the fear.
  4. Biggest pains. What's eating at you most right now — in relationships, health, money, work? Then rank them.
  5. The 6-month plan. Take your top three from each question above. With only six months to improve each, what would you do? End with one thing you can do today.

The most important line you'll write is the daily action at the end of question five. Keep it small and concrete — one call, one search, one email. Values become real the moment they turn into action.

Frequently asked questions

What is a personal value, really?

A value is something you do automatically, without needing motivation. It shows up in your actual behaviour — how you spend time, money, and energy — not in what you say you believe.

How do you find your true values?

Watch what you do, not what you say. Answering five clarity questions every day for two weeks — without rereading yesterday's answers — reveals the repeating patterns that are your real values.

Why answer the questions daily instead of once?

Answering once gives you a snapshot shaped by today's mood. Answering daily for two weeks surfaces the patterns that persist — those are the values that actually drive you.
